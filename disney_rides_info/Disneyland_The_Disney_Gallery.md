# The Disney Gallery
**Location:** Disneyland

## Overview
The Disney Gallery was an art gallery located above the Pirates of the Caribbean attraction in New Orleans Square. It showcased concept art, sketches, and limited-edition merchandise related to Disney films, theme park attractions, and the legacy of Walt Disney. The gallery provided a space for guests to appreciate the artistic development behind Disney's creative endeavors.

## Narrative and Theming
The gallery's theming was intended to evoke a sense of refined artistic appreciation. The space was designed to resemble a classic art museum, with ornate frames, elegant lighting, and a quiet atmosphere. The exhibits changed periodically, offering guests new perspectives on Disney's creative processes and celebrating various milestones in the company's history. The location above Pirates of the Caribbean provided a thematic connection to the park's immersive storytelling.

## Attraction Experience
Guests entered the gallery via a staircase located near the entrance to Pirates of the Caribbean. Inside, they could view rotating exhibits of artwork, including paintings, sculptures, and maquettes. The gallery also featured a retail space where guests could purchase limited-edition prints, collectibles, and other merchandise. The duration of a visit varied depending on the guest's interest in the displayed artwork and merchandise.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Disney Gallery was accessible to all guests. There were no height restrictions or specific health warnings associated with visiting the gallery. The attraction was suitable for guests of all ages who had an interest in art, Disney history, and the creative process behind Disney's productions.
