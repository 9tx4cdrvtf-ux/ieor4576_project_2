# Orbitron®
**Location:** Disneyland Park Paris

## Overview
Orbitron® is a spinner ride located in the Discoveryland area of Disneyland Park Paris. It is a variation of the Astro Orbiter attraction found in other Disney parks. The ride allows guests to pilot individual rockets that rotate around a central, planetary-themed structure. The attraction is manufactured by Zamperla. It provides panoramic views of Discoveryland and the surrounding park.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction is themed to represent a futuristic spaceport. Guests board individual rockets designed to resemble spacecraft. The central structure features a series of planetary models, including rings and other celestial bodies. The overall aesthetic is inspired by the retro-futuristic vision of the future, common in the works of Jules Verne and other science fiction authors. The ride's setting is intended to immerse guests in a world of space exploration and discovery.

## Attraction Experience
Guests queue in a line that winds through the attraction's base. Upon boarding, each rocket can be controlled by a joystick, allowing riders to adjust their altitude. The rockets rotate around the central structure, providing views of Discoveryland. The ride cycle lasts approximately 90 seconds. The attraction utilizes a combination of mechanical and electrical systems to provide the ride experience.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Orbitron® is generally suitable for guests of all ages, although young children may require assistance from an adult. There is no height requirement. Guests with heart conditions, back problems, or other health concerns should consider the ride's spinning motion before boarding. Pregnant women are advised against riding.
