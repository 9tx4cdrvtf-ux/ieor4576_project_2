# Stave Church Gallery
**Location:** Epcot

## Overview
The Stave Church Gallery is a walk-through exhibit located within the Norway Pavilion at Epcot's World Showcase. It showcases a replica of a 12th-century stave church, offering guests a glimpse into Norwegian history, culture, and architecture. The exhibit features artifacts, informational displays, and artwork related to Norse mythology and Norwegian heritage.

## Narrative and Theming
The gallery is themed to resemble a traditional Norwegian stave church, characterized by its intricate wooden construction and steeply pitched roof. The exhibit provides a historical context for the church's design, highlighting its significance as a center of community and religious life in medieval Norway. Displays explain the evolution of stave church architecture and its unique blend of Christian and pagan influences.

## Attraction Experience
Guests enter the gallery to explore the interior of the replica church. Inside, they can view exhibits featuring historical artifacts, including Viking-era objects and examples of traditional Norwegian craftsmanship. Informational panels provide details about the history of stave churches, Norse mythology, and the cultural practices of the Norwegian people. The exhibit is self-guided, allowing guests to explore at their own pace. The duration of the experience is approximately 10-15 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Stave Church Gallery is suitable for all ages. There are no height restrictions or specific health warnings associated with this attraction. Guests are encouraged to walk at their own pace and to be mindful of other visitors within the gallery.
