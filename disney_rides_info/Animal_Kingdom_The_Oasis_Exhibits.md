# The Oasis Exhibits
**Location:** Animal Kingdom

## Overview
The Oasis Exhibits is a series of interconnected animal habitats located at the entrance of Disney's Animal Kingdom. It serves as a transition zone, introducing guests to the park's themes of conservation and animal appreciation. The exhibits feature various animal species in naturalistic settings, allowing for close-up viewing and providing educational opportunities. The attraction is a self-guided walking experience, designed and maintained by Walt Disney Imagineering.

## Narrative and Theming
The Oasis is themed as a lush, tropical environment representing a variety of ecosystems. The exhibits are designed to mimic natural habitats, with carefully chosen plants, rockwork, and water features. There is no overarching narrative beyond the presentation of the animals and their environments. The theming emphasizes the importance of biodiversity and the interconnectedness of all living things.

## Attraction Experience
Guests traverse a series of pathways that wind through the different exhibits. Each exhibit showcases a specific animal species, such as macaws, wallabies, and various other birds and mammals. Viewing areas are strategically placed to offer optimal viewing opportunities. Informational signage provides details about the animals' behavior, habitat, and conservation status. The experience is self-paced, allowing guests to spend as much time as they wish at each exhibit. The duration of the experience varies depending on the guest's pace and interest.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Oasis Exhibits are suitable for all ages. There are no height restrictions or specific health warnings. Guests with mobility issues may find some pathways challenging due to uneven terrain. Strollers and wheelchairs are permitted.
