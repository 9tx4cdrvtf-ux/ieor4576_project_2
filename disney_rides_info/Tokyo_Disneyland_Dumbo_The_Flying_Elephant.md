# Dumbo The Flying Elephant
**Location:** Tokyo Disneyland

## Overview
Dumbo The Flying Elephant is a classic aerial carousel attraction located in Fantasyland at Tokyo Disneyland. It is a spinner ride, manufactured by Zamperla, based on the 1941 Disney animated film *Dumbo*. The attraction allows guests to experience the sensation of flight in a themed environment, and is a popular family-friendly ride.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction's setting is inspired by the circus environment depicted in the film. Guests board individual Dumbo the Elephant vehicles, each capable of accommodating up to two riders. The ride area is adorned with circus-themed decorations, including colorful banners and a central hub featuring a statue of Dumbo. The surrounding area is landscaped to enhance the whimsical atmosphere.

## Attraction Experience
Guests queue in a shaded area before boarding their chosen Dumbo vehicle. Once seated, riders can control the vertical movement of their elephant by using a lever inside the vehicle. The ride cycle lasts approximately 1 minute and 30 seconds. During the ride, the Dumbos rotate around a central axis, providing views of Fantasyland. The attraction utilizes simple mechanical technology to achieve its flight simulation.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Dumbo The Flying Elephant is designed for a general audience, particularly families with young children. There are no height restrictions. Guests with certain medical conditions, such as those prone to motion sickness, may wish to consider the ride's effects before participating. The attraction is accessible to guests using wheelchairs, with specific boarding procedures in place.
