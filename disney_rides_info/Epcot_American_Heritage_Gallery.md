# American Heritage Gallery
**Location:** Epcot

## Overview
The American Heritage Gallery was a walk-through exhibit located in the American Adventure pavilion at Epcot's World Showcase. It showcased a rotating collection of art and artifacts related to American history and culture. The gallery provided guests with a static, educational experience, offering insights into various periods and aspects of the United States' past.

## Narrative and Theming
The gallery's theming was directly tied to the American Adventure pavilion, presenting a curated selection of items intended to complement the pavilion's main attraction, a theatrical Audio-Animatronics show. The exhibit's setting was designed to evoke a sense of historical significance, with displays often featuring period-appropriate lighting, music, and informational signage. The goal was to provide a deeper understanding of the people, events, and artistic expressions that have shaped the American identity.

## Attraction Experience
Guests would enter the gallery and proceed through a series of display cases and exhibits. The content varied over time, but typically included historical documents, artwork, and artifacts. Informational panels provided context and details about each item. The experience was self-guided, allowing guests to explore at their own pace. The duration of a visit varied depending on individual interest, but typically lasted between 15 and 30 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The American Heritage Gallery was suitable for all ages. There were no height restrictions or specific health warnings associated with the exhibit, as it was a walk-through experience. Guests with mobility issues could navigate the gallery, though accessibility may have varied depending on the specific exhibit layout.
