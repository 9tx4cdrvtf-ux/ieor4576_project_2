# Space Mountain
**Location:** Disney Magic Kingdom

## Overview
Space Mountain is an enclosed, steel roller coaster located in Tomorrowland at the Magic Kingdom. It is one of the park's original attractions, opening in 1975, and is a significant landmark within the park. The attraction is notable for its indoor setting and use of darkness to enhance the sensation of speed and space travel.

## Narrative and Theming
Guests are invited to board rockets for a simulated journey through outer space. The queue area is designed to resemble a space station, complete with futuristic architecture, control panels, and ambient sound effects. The ride's narrative focuses on a voyage through a nebula, past shooting stars, and through asteroid fields. The darkness within the ride building contributes to the illusion of being in the vastness of space.

## Attraction Experience
Riders board individual rockets in a continuous-chain system. The ride features a series of drops, turns, and dips, all experienced in near-total darkness. Special effects, including projected stars and simulated meteor showers, are used to enhance the space travel experience. The ride duration is approximately 3 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Space Mountain is generally suitable for guests of all ages who meet the height requirement of 44 inches (112 cm). Guests with a history of heart conditions, back or neck problems, or those who are pregnant are advised against riding. The ride's enclosed nature and darkness may also be unsuitable for guests with claustrophobia or a sensitivity to flashing lights.
