# Palais du Cinéma
**Location:** Epcot

## Overview
Palais du Cinéma was a 360-degree film attraction located in the France Pavilion at Epcot's World Showcase. It presented a continuous, cinematic experience showcasing various aspects of French culture and history. The attraction utilized a circular theater with multiple screens, immersing guests in a panoramic view. It was a staple of the France Pavilion for many years, offering a glimpse into French life and landscapes.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction's narrative focused on a visual journey through France. The film presented iconic landmarks, cultural events, and everyday life scenes, aiming to evoke a sense of place and appreciation for French heritage. The theater's architecture, designed to resemble a classic Parisian cinema, further enhanced the immersive experience. The setting was designed to transport guests to France, offering a brief escape into the country's beauty and charm.

## Attraction Experience
Guests entered a circular theater and stood while the film played on the surrounding screens. The film, typically lasting approximately 18 minutes, utilized multiple synchronized projectors to create a seamless, panoramic view. The film's content varied over the years, but consistently featured scenes of French cities, countryside, and cultural events. The experience was primarily visual, with no physical ride elements.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Palais du Cinéma was suitable for all ages. There were no height restrictions. The attraction was accessible to guests using wheelchairs. Due to the continuous nature of the film, guests prone to motion sickness should exercise caution.
