# Living with the Land
**Location:** Epcot

## Overview
Living with the Land is a slow-moving boat ride attraction located within The Land pavilion at Epcot in Walt Disney World. The attraction, developed by Walt Disney Imagineering, offers guests a glimpse into the history and future of agriculture. It combines a gentle boat ride with educational displays and working greenhouses, showcasing innovative farming techniques.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction presents a journey through various ecosystems and agricultural practices. Guests begin by observing a simulated rainforest, prairie, and desert environment. The ride then transitions to a tour of working greenhouses, where various crops are cultivated using different methods, including hydroponics and integrated pest management. The overall theme emphasizes the importance of sustainable agriculture and the potential for feeding a growing global population.

## Attraction Experience
Guests board a boat that travels along a water-based track. The ride begins with a diorama-style presentation of different biomes. The boat then enters the greenhouses, where guests observe various crops, including fruits, vegetables, and plants. The ride showcases different farming techniques, including innovative methods developed by Disney's horticulturalists. The entire experience lasts approximately 14 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Living with the Land is designed for a general audience and is suitable for all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests with mobility issues can remain in their wheelchairs or ECVs. The ride is a gentle experience and does not contain any intense visual or auditory effects.
