# Roger Rabbit's Car Toon Spin
**Location:** Disneyland

## Overview
Roger Rabbit's Car Toon Spin is a dark ride located in Mickey's Toontown at Disneyland. The attraction is a "trackless" spinning dark ride, manufactured by Walt Disney Imagineering. It opened in 1994, coinciding with the opening of Mickey's Toontown, and is based on the 1988 film *Who Framed Roger Rabbit*. The ride is a significant component of Toontown's immersive environment, offering guests a whimsical and interactive experience.

## Narrative and Theming
Guests are immersed in the world of Toontown, specifically the setting of the film. The queue line winds through a cartoon cityscape, featuring various gags and references to the movie. The premise involves guests becoming taxi drivers in Benny the Cab's fleet, tasked with helping Roger Rabbit evade the villainous Judge Doom and the weasels. The environment is designed to resemble a vibrant, animated world, with exaggerated architecture and cartoon sound effects.

## Attraction Experience
Guests board a taxi cab that seats up to three riders. The vehicles travel along a trackless system, allowing for unpredictable movements and spinning. The ride incorporates various animatronics, special effects, and projected imagery to depict scenes from the film. Riders can manually spin their vehicles, increasing their control over the experience. The ride lasts approximately 3 minutes, navigating through scenes such as the Ink & Paint Club and the Acme warehouse.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The attraction is generally suitable for all ages, though young children may require parental supervision. There is no height requirement. Guests with a history of motion sickness or other health concerns should consider the spinning nature of the ride before boarding.
