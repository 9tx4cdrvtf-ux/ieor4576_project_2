# Tree of Life
**Location:** Animal Kingdom

## Overview
The Tree of Life is a sculpted artificial baobab tree and the central icon of Disney's Animal Kingdom theme park. It serves as a visual centerpiece and houses several attractions. The tree is not a ride in itself, but rather a landmark that provides the setting for a 360-degree theater show, "It's Tough to be a Bug!" The Tree of Life was designed and constructed by Walt Disney Imagineering.

## Narrative and Theming
The Tree of Life is intricately carved with over 300 animal carvings, representing the interconnectedness of all living creatures. The tree's design is inspired by the baobab trees of Africa, and its carvings depict a variety of animals, from insects to mammals. The surrounding area is designed to resemble a lush, natural environment, reinforcing the park's theme of celebrating the natural world.

## Attraction Experience
"It's Tough to be a Bug!" is a 3D film experience presented within the Tree of Life's trunk. Guests enter a theater and are seated on benches that move and vibrate in sync with the on-screen action. The show incorporates various sensory effects, including spraying water, air blasts, and simulated insect encounters. The show's duration is approximately 8 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The attraction is generally suitable for all ages. There are no height restrictions. However, the show contains loud noises, sudden movements, and potentially frightening imagery, which may not be suitable for very young children or those sensitive to such effects. Guests with back or neck problems should consider the ride's motion effects before entering.
