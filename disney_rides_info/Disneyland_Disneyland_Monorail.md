# Disneyland Monorail
**Location:** Disneyland

## Overview
The Disneyland Monorail is an elevated, mass-transit system operating within Disneyland Park in Anaheim, California. It is a monorail system, a type of railway system in which the track consists of a single rail. The Disneyland Monorail, originally designed by WED Enterprises (now Walt Disney Imagineering) and built by ALWEG, opened in 1959, making it the first daily operating monorail in the Western Hemisphere. It serves as both a transportation system and an attraction, offering guests scenic views of Tomorrowland and other areas of the park.

## Narrative and Theming
The Monorail's narrative centers on futuristic transportation. Guests are presented with a glimpse into the future of travel, experiencing a smooth, elevated ride above the park. The sleek design of the trains and the elevated track contribute to the futuristic aesthetic, reinforcing the Tomorrowland theme. The ride provides a unique perspective of the park, allowing guests to observe attractions and landscapes from above.

## Attraction Experience
Guests board the Monorail at either the Tomorrowland station or the Downtown Disney station. The trains travel along a 2.5-mile (4.0 km) elevated beamway, providing a 13-minute round trip. The ride offers views of Tomorrowland, Fantasyland, and Critter Country, as well as glimpses of backstage areas. The trains are fully automated and controlled by a central system. The ride is smooth and quiet, with announcements providing information about the surrounding attractions.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Disneyland Monorail is suitable for guests of all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests with mobility issues can be accommodated. Guests with a fear of heights should be aware that the ride is elevated.
