# La Cabane des Robinson
**Location:** Disneyland Park Paris

## Overview
La Cabane des Robinson (Robinson's Treehouse) is a walk-through attraction located in Adventureland at Disneyland Park Paris. It is themed after the Swiss Family Robinson, a novel by Johann David Wyss, and allows guests to explore a multi-level treehouse constructed by the fictional Robinson family. The attraction provides a unique perspective of Adventureland and offers detailed theming and immersive environments.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction immerses guests in the story of the Robinson family, shipwrecked and forced to build a home in a large tree. The treehouse is depicted as a self-sufficient dwelling, utilizing salvaged materials and natural resources. The theming emphasizes ingenuity and resourcefulness, showcasing various living areas, workshops, and observation points. The environment is filled with details, including furniture, tools, and decorative elements, all designed to reflect the family's life.

## Attraction Experience
Guests ascend a series of wooden staircases and rope bridges to explore the various levels of the treehouse. The route winds through several rooms, including a kitchen, dining area, and bedrooms. The attraction features detailed props and animatronics, such as a water wheel and a pulley system, to enhance the immersive experience. The walk-through concludes at the highest point, offering panoramic views of Adventureland and the surrounding areas. The entire experience typically lasts approximately 10-15 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
La Cabane des Robinson is suitable for guests of all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests should be prepared to climb stairs and navigate uneven surfaces. The attraction is accessible via a ramp for guests with mobility impairments, though some areas may be difficult to access. There are no specific health warnings or restrictions.
