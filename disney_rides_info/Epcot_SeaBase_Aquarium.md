# SeaBase Aquarium
**Location:** Epcot

## Overview
SeaBase Aquarium, officially known as The Seas with Nemo & Friends, is an aquarium located within the Land Pavilion at Epcot in Walt Disney World. It is one of the largest aquariums in North America, housing thousands of marine animals representing over 60 species. The attraction combines educational exhibits with immersive experiences, offering guests a glimpse into diverse aquatic ecosystems.

## Narrative and Theming
The SeaBase Aquarium is themed as a research facility dedicated to the study and conservation of marine life. Guests are invited to explore various habitats, including coral reefs, open oceans, and the polar regions. The narrative is interwoven with the characters and themes from the Disney-Pixar film *Finding Nemo*, enhancing the experience for younger audiences. The overall atmosphere is designed to be educational and engaging, promoting awareness of ocean conservation.

## Attraction Experience
Guests can explore the aquarium through various viewing windows and tunnels. The main attraction is the observation of the diverse marine life, including dolphins, sea turtles, sharks, and various species of fish. Interactive exhibits provide additional information about the animals and their habitats. The attraction also features a slow-moving Omnimover ride, "The Seas with Nemo & Friends," which takes guests through scenes from the film before arriving at the aquarium. The entire experience typically lasts approximately 1-2 hours.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
SeaBase Aquarium is suitable for all ages. There are no height restrictions. The attraction is accessible to guests with disabilities. No specific health warnings or restrictions are in place.
