# Tower of Terror
**Location:** Tokyo DisneySea

## Overview
Tower of Terror is a drop tower dark ride attraction located in the American Waterfront area of Tokyo DisneySea. It is a unique version of the attraction, distinct from the versions found at other Disney parks. The ride is themed around a cursed hotel and utilizes a vertical lift system to simulate a freefall experience.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction is themed around the Hotel Hightower, a luxurious hotel owned by the eccentric and wealthy explorer, Harrison Hightower. Hightower, known for his obsession with collecting artifacts, mysteriously disappeared on New Year's Eve in 1899 after acquiring a cursed idol. Guests are invited to tour the hotel, now abandoned and rumored to be haunted, and learn about Hightower's final, fateful journey. The queue line and pre-show extensively develop the backstory, immersing guests in the hotel's opulent, yet decaying, atmosphere.

## Attraction Experience
Guests board a service elevator that ascends through the hotel. The ride incorporates various special effects, including projections, animatronics, and lighting, to enhance the storytelling. The elevator then enters a series of vertical drops, providing the sensation of freefall. The ride experience lasts approximately three minutes, including the pre-show and the elevator sequence.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The attraction is suitable for guests of most ages, although it is recommended for those who are comfortable with heights and sudden drops. Guests must be at least 102 cm (40 inches) tall to ride. Individuals with heart conditions, back or neck problems, or those who are pregnant are advised against riding.
