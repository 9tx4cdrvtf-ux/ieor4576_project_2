# Walt Disney's Carousel of Progress
**Location:** Disney Magic Kingdom

## Overview
Walt Disney's Carousel of Progress is a rotating theater attraction located in Tomorrowland at the Magic Kingdom. It is a theatrical presentation showcasing the evolution of technology and its impact on American family life throughout the 20th century. Originally created for the 1964-65 New York World's Fair, it is a significant example of Audio-Animatronics and a testament to Walt Disney's vision for the future.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction presents a fictional American family – John, his wife, and their children – as they experience technological advancements across different eras. The setting transitions through four distinct scenes: the early 1900s, the 1920s, the 1940s, and the present day. Each scene depicts the family interacting with new inventions, highlighting the improvements in their daily lives. The show is accompanied by the iconic song "There's a Great Big Beautiful Tomorrow."

## Attraction Experience
Guests are seated in a circular theater that rotates around a series of stationary stages. As the theater moves, each stage is revealed, showcasing a different scene. Audio-Animatronic figures perform in each scene, interacting with the environment and narrating the story. The entire show lasts approximately 20 minutes, including the transition times between scenes. The attraction utilizes a combination of lighting, sound effects, and projections to enhance the immersive experience.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Carousel of Progress is suitable for all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests with mobility issues should be aware that they must transfer from a wheelchair or ECV to a standard theater seat. The attraction is generally considered a gentle ride and does not contain any intense visual or auditory effects.
