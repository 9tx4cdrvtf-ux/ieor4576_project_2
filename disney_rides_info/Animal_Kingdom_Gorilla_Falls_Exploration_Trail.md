# Gorilla Falls Exploration Trail
**Location:** Animal Kingdom

## Overview
The Gorilla Falls Exploration Trail is a self-guided walking trail located in the Africa section of Disney's Animal Kingdom. It is designed to showcase a variety of African wildlife in naturalistic habitats. The trail allows guests to observe animals such as gorillas, okapi, meerkats, and various bird species. The attraction emphasizes conservation and education, providing information about the animals and their environments.

## Narrative and Theming
The trail is themed to resemble a research outpost and conservation area within an African savanna and forest environment. Signage and informational displays along the path provide details about the animals' behaviors, habitats, and conservation status. The immersive environment includes lush vegetation, themed rockwork, and carefully designed viewing areas to enhance the guest experience and create a sense of discovery.

## Attraction Experience
Guests begin the trail by entering a shaded pathway, progressing through various animal habitats. The primary highlight is the gorilla habitat, featuring a troop of western lowland gorillas. Viewing areas offer opportunities for close-up observation. Along the trail, guests encounter other animal species, including a hippo viewing area, a meerkat exhibit, and a bird aviary. The duration of the experience varies depending on the guest's pace and interest in the exhibits, typically lasting between 20 to 30 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Gorilla Falls Exploration Trail is suitable for guests of all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests with mobility issues may find some areas challenging due to uneven terrain. Strollers are permitted. There are no specific health warnings or restrictions, but guests should be aware of the outdoor environment and the potential for sun exposure.
