# Soaring Over the Horizon
**Location:** Shanghai Disney Resort

## Overview
Soaring Over the Horizon is a simulated flight attraction located in the Gardens of Imagination area of Shanghai Disneyland. It is a motion-based, flight-simulator ride system manufactured by Dynamic Attractions, similar to the original "Soarin'" attraction at Disney California Adventure and Epcot. The attraction allows guests to experience a simulated flight over various global landmarks. It is a significant draw for the park, known for its immersive visuals and sensory effects.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction's narrative centers around the concept of a "flight" experience. Guests are presented with the premise of joining a journey across the world, taking in iconic locations. The queue area is themed to resemble an airport terminal, complete with pre-flight briefings and travel posters. This theming establishes the context for the simulated flight, preparing guests for the journey ahead.

## Attraction Experience
Guests are seated in a suspended theater with multiple rows of seats. The theater then rises, positioning riders in front of a massive, curved screen. The ride utilizes a combination of motion, wind, scents, and lighting effects to simulate the sensation of flight. The film showcases various global locations, including the Great Wall of China, the Eiffel Tower, and the Matterhorn. The ride duration is approximately five minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Soaring Over the Horizon is generally suitable for a wide audience. The height requirement is 102 cm (40 inches). Guests with pre-existing health conditions, particularly those susceptible to motion sickness, or with back or neck problems, are advised to consider their condition before riding. Expectant mothers are also advised against riding.
