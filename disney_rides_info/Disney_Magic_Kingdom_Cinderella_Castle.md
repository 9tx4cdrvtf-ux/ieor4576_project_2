# Cinderella Castle
**Location:** Disney Magic Kingdom

## Overview
Cinderella Castle is a fairytale-inspired castle located in the Magic Kingdom at Walt Disney World in Bay Lake, Florida. It serves as an iconic symbol of the park and the Walt Disney Company, representing the heart of the Magic Kingdom. The castle is a walk-through attraction and a visual centerpiece, designed by Walt Disney Imagineering. It does not have a specific manufacturer.

## Narrative and Theming
The castle's design is based on the fairytale of Cinderella, drawing inspiration from various European castles. It is designed to appear larger than life through forced perspective, with architectural details becoming smaller towards the top. The interior features mosaics depicting the Cinderella story, as well as a restaurant, Cinderella's Royal Table, and various shops. The castle's exterior is regularly used for nighttime projections and fireworks displays, enhancing the immersive experience.

## Attraction Experience
Guests can walk through the castle's interior, viewing the mosaics that tell the story of Cinderella. The mosaics are made of Venetian glass tiles and depict key scenes from the animated film. The experience is self-guided and takes approximately 15-20 minutes to complete. The castle also houses Cinderella's Royal Table, a character dining experience that requires reservations.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Cinderella Castle is accessible to all guests. There are no height restrictions or specific health warnings associated with the walk-through experience. However, guests with mobility issues may find navigating the interior challenging due to stairs.
