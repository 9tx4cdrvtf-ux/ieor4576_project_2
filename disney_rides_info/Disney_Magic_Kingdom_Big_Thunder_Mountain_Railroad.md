# Big Thunder Mountain Railroad
**Location:** Disney Magic Kingdom

## Overview
Big Thunder Mountain Railroad is a mine train roller coaster located in Frontierland at Disney's Magic Kingdom. It is a steel roller coaster designed and manufactured by Walt Disney Imagineering. The attraction is a significant element of Frontierland, contributing to the park's immersive theming and offering a thrilling, family-friendly experience.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction's narrative centers around a runaway mine train through the fictional town of Tumbleweed, located in the American Southwest. Guests are transported to the late 1800s, where they are purportedly riding through the remnants of a gold rush boomtown. The mountain itself is themed to resemble a red rock landscape, with abandoned mining equipment, geological formations, and a variety of animated figures, including bats and a runaway train. The lore suggests the mountain is cursed, leading to the train's erratic behavior.

## Attraction Experience
Guests board a mine train car and are dispatched on a winding track through the mountain. The ride features several lift hills and drops, navigating through caverns, around rock formations, and past various themed scenes. The ride incorporates special effects, including simulated explosions, lighting effects, and audio-animatronics. The ride duration is approximately 3 minutes and 30 seconds.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Big Thunder Mountain Railroad is designed for a broad audience, including families. Guests must be at least 40 inches (102 cm) tall to ride. Guests with a history of heart conditions, back or neck problems, or those who are pregnant are advised to consider their health before riding.
