# Mark Twain Riverboat
**Location:** Tokyo Disneyland

## Overview
The Mark Twain Riverboat is a large-scale, operating paddle steamer attraction located in the Rivers of America area of Tokyo Disneyland's Westernland. It is a scenic boat ride offering guests a leisurely tour of the river, providing views of various themed environments and animatronic figures. The vessel is a replica of a classic Mississippi riverboat and is a staple attraction in several Disney theme parks worldwide.

## Narrative and Theming
Guests board the Mark Twain Riverboat and embark on a journey reminiscent of the 19th-century American frontier. The boat's design and décor evoke the era of riverboat travel, with details such as ornate railings, period-accurate furnishings, and the sounds of steam whistles. The Rivers of America itself is themed to represent the American frontier, with sights including Native American villages, wildlife scenes, and other elements designed to transport guests to a bygone era.

## Attraction Experience
The ride lasts approximately 12 minutes. The Mark Twain Riverboat slowly circumnavigates the Rivers of America. Guests are seated on multiple decks, offering views of the surrounding scenery. The boat is propelled by a combination of electric motors and a working paddlewheel, providing a realistic visual and auditory experience. The route passes by various animatronic figures and themed set pieces, enhancing the storytelling experience.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Mark Twain Riverboat is suitable for guests of all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests with mobility issues may find access challenging due to the boat's design and the need to navigate stairs. Guests with a fear of heights or enclosed spaces should consider their comfort levels before riding.
