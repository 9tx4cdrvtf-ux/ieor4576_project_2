# Swiss Family Treehouse
**Location:** Disney Magic Kingdom

## Overview
The Swiss Family Treehouse is a walk-through attraction located in Adventureland at the Magic Kingdom. It allows guests to explore a multi-level, detailed recreation of the treehouse featured in the 1960 Walt Disney film, *Swiss Family Robinson*. The attraction is a non-ride experience, offering a unique perspective of the park and a detailed example of themed environmental storytelling.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction immerses guests in the fictional world of the Robinson family, shipwrecked and forced to build a home in a large, ancient tree. The treehouse is presented as a testament to the family's ingenuity and resourcefulness, constructed from salvaged materials and incorporating natural elements. The theming emphasizes the family's ability to adapt and thrive in a challenging environment, showcasing various living spaces, storage areas, and defensive measures.

## Attraction Experience
Guests ascend the treehouse via a series of wooden staircases and rope bridges, navigating through multiple levels. The experience involves a self-guided tour, allowing visitors to observe the various rooms and features of the treehouse, including the kitchen, living room, and sleeping quarters. The attraction incorporates detailed props and set dressing, such as water wheels, pulley systems, and furniture crafted from natural materials. The entire experience typically lasts approximately 10-15 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Swiss Family Treehouse is suitable for guests of all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests should be aware that the attraction involves climbing multiple flights of stairs and navigating uneven surfaces. Individuals with mobility issues or a fear of heights may find the attraction challenging.
