# Wildlife Express Train
**Location:** Animal Kingdom

## Overview
The Wildlife Express Train is a narrow-gauge railroad attraction located within Disney's Animal Kingdom theme park. It provides transportation between the park's Africa section and Rafiki's Planet Watch. The train is a simple, open-air ride, manufactured by the Walt Disney Imagineering and built by the Philadelphia Toboggan Coasters. It serves primarily as a mode of transport, offering guests a behind-the-scenes glimpse of the park's operations and animal care facilities.

## Narrative and Theming
The train ride is presented as a journey to Rafiki's Planet Watch, a conservation and education center. The narrative suggests a trip to a research and animal care facility. The train's route passes through backstage areas, offering views of animal holding areas, veterinary facilities, and food preparation areas. The theming is consistent with the park's overall aesthetic, incorporating elements of African architecture and design.

## Attraction Experience
Guests board the train at the Africa station. The train travels approximately 1.2 miles (1.9 km) to Rafiki's Planet Watch, a journey that takes about seven minutes. The ride is a simple, slow-moving experience. The train makes one stop at Rafiki's Planet Watch, allowing guests to disembark and explore the exhibits. The train then returns to the Africa station, completing the round trip.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Wildlife Express Train is suitable for all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests with mobility issues may find the boarding process challenging. Guests with health concerns should consult with a medical professional before riding.
