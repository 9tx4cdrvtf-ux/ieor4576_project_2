# Mission: SPACE
**Location:** Epcot

## Overview
Mission: SPACE is a centrifugal motion simulator attraction located in the World Discovery section of Epcot at Walt Disney World. The ride simulates a mission to Mars, utilizing a combination of motion, visual projections, and sound effects to create an immersive experience. The attraction was developed by Walt Disney Imagineering and utilizes technology from Vekoma. It is known for its intense motion and is a significant thrill ride within the park.

## Narrative and Theming
Guests are recruited as astronauts for the International Space Training Center (ISTC) and are briefed on their mission to Mars. The queue area features interactive displays and pre-show videos that further immerse guests in the narrative, explaining the mission's objectives and the technology involved. The overall theme is a realistic depiction of a space training facility, preparing guests for their simulated journey.

## Attraction Experience
Guests board a capsule-like vehicle and are assigned roles within the simulated spacecraft. The ride utilizes a centrifugal motion simulator to create the sensation of acceleration, weightlessness, and other forces experienced during space travel. The experience includes visual projections on a large screen, simulating the journey to Mars, and audio effects that enhance the realism. The "Orange" version of the ride features more intense motion, while the "Green" version offers a less intense experience. The entire experience lasts approximately five minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Mission: SPACE is designed for guests who are at least 40 inches (102 cm) tall. Due to the intense motion and potential for motion sickness, guests with a history of these issues, heart problems, back or neck problems, or those who are pregnant are strongly advised against riding. Guests with claustrophobia may also find the enclosed ride vehicle uncomfortable.
