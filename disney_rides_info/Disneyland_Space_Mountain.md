# Space Mountain
**Location:** Disneyland

## Overview
Space Mountain is an enclosed, space-themed steel roller coaster located in Tomorrowland at Disneyland. It is one of the park's most popular attractions and a significant example of a dark ride, utilizing a combination of roller coaster elements and immersive theming. The original Space Mountain, designed and built by Walt Disney Imagineering, opened in 1977.

## Narrative and Theming
Guests are launched into a simulated journey through outer space, aboard rockets designed to explore the cosmos. The queue area features futuristic architecture and sound effects, creating an atmosphere of anticipation. Inside the ride building, the darkness is punctuated by constellations, nebulas, and other celestial bodies, enhancing the illusion of interstellar travel. The ride's narrative focuses on the thrill of space exploration and the unknown dangers of the cosmos.

## Attraction Experience
Guests board rockets that ascend a lift hill into the darkness. The coaster then navigates a series of drops, turns, and twists, all within the enclosed space. The ride utilizes a combination of lighting effects, sound design, and projections to simulate the sensation of traveling through space. The ride duration is approximately 2 minutes and 30 seconds.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Space Mountain is generally suitable for guests of all ages who meet the height requirement of 40 inches (102 cm). Guests with a history of heart conditions, back or neck problems, or those who are pregnant are advised against riding. The ride's enclosed nature and darkness may also be unsuitable for guests with claustrophobia or a sensitivity to flashing lights.
