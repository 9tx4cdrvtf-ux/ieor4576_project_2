# Frontierland Shootin' Exposition
**Location:** Disneyland

## Overview
Frontierland Shootin' Exposition is a shooting gallery attraction located in Frontierland at Disneyland. It is a stationary, target-shooting game where guests use replica rifles to aim at various animated targets. The attraction is a classic example of a midway-style game adapted for a theme park environment and has remained a popular activity since its opening.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction is themed to the American Old West, consistent with the overall aesthetic of Frontierland. Guests are invited to test their marksmanship skills in a simulated frontier shooting range. The setting features a backdrop of a Western town, complete with animated targets depicting various animals, objects, and humorous scenarios. The environment is designed to evoke the atmosphere of a frontier shooting gallery, common in the 19th-century American West.

## Attraction Experience
Guests purchase ammunition (typically in the form of tokens) to operate the replica rifles. The rifles are mounted on a counter and fire infrared beams at the targets. Upon hitting a target, the target reacts with movement, sound effects, and sometimes visual displays. The game is self-paced, and guests can shoot at any target they choose. The duration of the experience is dependent on the number of tokens purchased.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Frontierland Shootin' Exposition is suitable for guests of all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests with photosensitivity or sensitivity to loud noises should be aware of the potential for flashing lights and sound effects. There are no specific health warnings or restrictions.
