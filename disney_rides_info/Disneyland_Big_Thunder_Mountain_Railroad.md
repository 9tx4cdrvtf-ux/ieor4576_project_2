# Big Thunder Mountain Railroad
**Location:** Disneyland

## Overview
Big Thunder Mountain Railroad is a mine train roller coaster located in Frontierland at Disneyland. It opened in 1979 and was designed and built by Walt Disney Imagineering. The attraction is a significant element of Disneyland's immersive theming, offering a thrilling ride experience within a detailed Old West setting.

## Narrative and Theming
The ride's narrative centers around a haunted gold-mining town, Big Thunder Mountain, located in the American Southwest. Guests are transported back in time to the late 1800s, boarding a runaway mine train that navigates through the abandoned mineshafts and around the mountain. The theming incorporates elements of the supernatural, with references to cursed gold and ghostly apparitions, enhancing the immersive experience. The landscape includes desert scenery, rock formations, and the remnants of the mining operation.

## Attraction Experience
Guests board a train consisting of several cars designed to resemble mine train cars. The ride begins with a slow ascent up a lift hill, followed by a series of drops, turns, and twists through the mountain. The coaster utilizes a combination of physical sets, animatronics, and special effects, including simulated explosions and lighting effects, to create a dynamic and engaging experience. The ride duration is approximately 3 minutes and 15 seconds.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Big Thunder Mountain Railroad is generally suitable for guests of all ages, though it is a thrilling ride. The minimum height requirement is 40 inches (102 cm). Guests with a history of heart conditions, back or neck problems, or those who are pregnant are advised to consider their health before riding.
