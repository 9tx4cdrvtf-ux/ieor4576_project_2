# Dumbo the Flying Elephant
**Location:** Disneyland

## Overview
Dumbo the Flying Elephant is a classic spinner ride located in Fantasyland at Disneyland. Based on the 1941 Disney animated film *Dumbo*, the attraction allows guests to experience the sensation of flight in a themed environment. It is a variation of the "flying carousel" ride type, manufactured by Arrow Development. The ride is a long-standing and iconic attraction, often considered a quintessential Disneyland experience.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction's narrative centers on the titular character, Dumbo, a young elephant who learns to fly. Guests board individual Dumbo-shaped vehicles, each capable of carrying up to two riders. The ride area is themed to resemble a circus environment, with colorful tents and whimsical decorations. The central hub features a large, animated statue of Dumbo.

## Attraction Experience
Guests queue in a line that often extends into a shaded area. Upon boarding, riders are seated in their Dumbo vehicle and can control the vertical movement of their elephant by using a lever inside the vehicle. The ride cycle lasts approximately 1.5 minutes. The ride vehicles rotate around a central axis, providing a gentle, aerial view of Fantasyland.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Dumbo the Flying Elephant is designed for a general audience, particularly families with young children. There are no height restrictions. Guests with a fear of heights or those prone to motion sickness should consider their comfort levels before riding. Expectant mothers and individuals with certain medical conditions should consult with a medical professional before riding.
