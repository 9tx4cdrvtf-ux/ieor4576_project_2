# Animal Care at Conservation Station
**Location:** Animal Kingdom

## Overview
Animal Care at Conservation Station is an educational attraction located in Rafiki's Planet Watch at Disney's Animal Kingdom. It offers guests a behind-the-scenes look at the veterinary care and animal husbandry practices employed by the park. The attraction provides insights into animal health, conservation efforts, and the daily routines of animal care professionals.

## Narrative and Theming
The Conservation Station is themed as a working animal care facility. Guests arrive via the Wildlife Express Train, which transports them to a remote area of the park. The station itself is designed to resemble a research and rehabilitation center, with various exhibits and viewing areas that showcase the work of veterinarians, animal keepers, and conservationists. The environment emphasizes the importance of animal welfare and global conservation initiatives.

## Attraction Experience
Upon entering the Conservation Station, guests can observe animal care procedures through viewing windows. These include veterinary examinations, feeding preparations, and animal training sessions. Interactive exhibits provide information on animal diets, habitats, and conservation projects. The attraction also features a live animal presentation area where guests can learn about specific species and their conservation status. The duration of a visit varies depending on guest interest, but typically lasts between 30 to 60 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Animal Care at Conservation Station is suitable for all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests with mobility issues should be aware that the Wildlife Express Train requires boarding and disembarking. The attraction is generally accessible to guests with disabilities; however, specific accommodations may vary.
