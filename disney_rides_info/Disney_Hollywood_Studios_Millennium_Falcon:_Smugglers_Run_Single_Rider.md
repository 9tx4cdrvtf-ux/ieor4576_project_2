# Millennium Falcon: Smugglers Run Single Rider
**Location:** Disney Hollywood Studios

## Overview
Millennium Falcon: Smugglers Run Single Rider is a motion simulator attraction located within Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge at Disney's Hollywood Studios. The ride allows guests to experience a simulated flight aboard the iconic Millennium Falcon starship. The attraction was developed by Walt Disney Imagineering and utilizes a motion simulator system to provide an immersive, interactive experience. It is a key component of the Star Wars-themed land, offering guests a chance to participate in a smuggling mission.

## Narrative and Theming
Guests are immersed in the Star Wars universe, specifically the era following *Star Wars: The Last Jedi*. The premise involves guests working as part of a crew hired by the space pirate, Hondo Ohnaka. The mission involves piloting the Millennium Falcon on a smuggling run, with each guest assigned a specific role: pilot, gunner, or engineer. The queue area is themed as Ohnaka Transport Solutions, a salvage and smuggling operation, with detailed props and environments that enhance the narrative.

## Attraction Experience
The Single Rider queue allows guests to bypass the standard queue and be placed in available positions within the ride vehicles. Guests are assigned roles and enter the cockpit of the Millennium Falcon. The ride utilizes a motion simulator synchronized with a projected screen, creating the illusion of flight. The experience lasts approximately five minutes, during which guests interact with the controls based on their assigned role, influencing the outcome of the mission. The ride incorporates visual effects, audio cues, and physical sensations to enhance immersion.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The attraction is suitable for a wide audience, though children under a certain height must be accompanied by a supervising companion. Guests must be at least 38 inches (97 cm) tall to ride. Guests with certain medical conditions, such as heart problems, back or neck issues, or those prone to motion sickness, are advised to consider the ride's intensity before boarding.
