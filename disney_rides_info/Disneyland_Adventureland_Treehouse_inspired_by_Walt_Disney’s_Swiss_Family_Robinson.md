# Adventureland Treehouse inspired by Walt Disney’s Swiss Family Robinson
**Location:** Disneyland

## Overview
The Adventureland Treehouse, formerly known as the Swiss Family Treehouse, is a walk-through attraction located in Adventureland at Disneyland. It is themed after the 1960 Walt Disney film *Swiss Family Robinson*. The attraction allows guests to explore a multi-level treehouse constructed by the Robinson family, showcasing their ingenuity and resourcefulness.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction immerses guests in the story of the Robinson family, shipwrecked on a tropical island. The treehouse is presented as their meticulously crafted home, built to survive the elements and provide comfort. The theming emphasizes the family's use of salvaged materials and natural resources, with various rooms and features demonstrating their innovative solutions for everyday living. The environment includes details such as a kitchen, living area, and sleeping quarters, all integrated into the structure of a large, mature tree.

## Attraction Experience
Guests ascend a series of wooden staircases and rope bridges to explore the treehouse. The walk-through experience allows visitors to observe various scenes and details, including water collection systems, a kitchen with cooking implements, and a dining area. Audio effects, such as the sounds of water flowing and animals, enhance the immersive experience. The attraction is self-guided, allowing guests to explore at their own pace. The duration of the experience is approximately 10-15 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Adventureland Treehouse is suitable for all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests should be aware that the attraction involves climbing stairs and navigating uneven surfaces. Guests with mobility issues may find the attraction challenging.
