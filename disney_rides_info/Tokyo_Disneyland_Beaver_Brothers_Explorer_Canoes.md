# Beaver Brothers Explorer Canoes
**Location:** Tokyo Disneyland

## Overview
Beaver Brothers Explorer Canoes is a water-based attraction located in the Westernland area of Tokyo Disneyland. It is a manually-powered canoe ride where guests navigate a waterway, offering scenic views and interactive elements. The attraction is a variation of the "canoe" rides found in other Disney parks, providing a unique, physical experience.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction immerses guests in the spirit of frontier exploration. The canoes are themed to resemble those used by early explorers and fur trappers in the American West. The waterway winds through a landscape designed to evoke the natural beauty of the region, with themed rockwork, foliage, and audio effects that enhance the sense of adventure and wilderness. The "Beaver Brothers" name refers to the fictional characters who are implied to have built and operate the canoes.

## Attraction Experience
Guests board large, open-air canoes, each accommodating multiple riders. The canoes are propelled by the guests themselves, using paddles provided by the attraction. The journey takes approximately 10-15 minutes, during which guests navigate a circular route around a themed island. The route features various scenic elements, including simulated waterfalls, wildlife animatronics, and themed environments. The experience is primarily visual and auditory, with no special effects beyond the natural sounds and scenery.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The attraction is suitable for guests of all ages and physical abilities, provided they are able to paddle. There are no height restrictions. Guests should be aware that they are actively participating in the propulsion of the canoe and may experience some physical exertion. Guests with mobility issues may require assistance.
