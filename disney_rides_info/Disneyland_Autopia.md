# Autopia
**Location:** Disneyland

## Overview
Autopia is a self-steer, track-guided vehicle ride located in Tomorrowland at Disneyland. It is one of the park's original attractions, opening with the park in 1955. The attraction allows guests to drive miniature gasoline-powered cars along a guided track, simulating the experience of driving a real automobile.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction presents a futuristic vision of transportation, reflecting the optimistic view of the future prevalent in the mid-20th century. The setting is a stylized highway system, complete with roadways, bridges, and roadside scenery. The vehicles themselves are designed to resemble contemporary automobiles, albeit scaled down for the attraction. The theming emphasizes the freedom and excitement of driving, albeit within a controlled environment.

## Attraction Experience
Guests queue and board individual two-seater cars. Once seated, the cars are started and proceed along a winding track. While the cars are equipped with steering wheels, they are guided by a center rail, preventing the vehicles from leaving the designated path. The ride lasts approximately 2-3 minutes, offering views of the surrounding Tomorrowland area and interactions with other Autopia vehicles.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Autopia is generally suitable for guests of all ages, although children must be tall enough to reach the accelerator pedal. There is a height requirement of 32 inches (81 cm) to ride. Guests with certain medical conditions, such as back or neck problems, or those who are pregnant, are advised to consider the ride's motion before participating.
