# Swiss Family Treehouse
**Location:** Tokyo Disneyland

## Overview
The Swiss Family Treehouse is a walk-through attraction located in Adventureland at Tokyo Disneyland. Based on the 1960 Disney film *Swiss Family Robinson*, the attraction allows guests to explore a multi-level treehouse constructed by the Robinson family after being shipwrecked. It is a non-thrill attraction designed to provide an immersive experience of the film's setting.

## Narrative and Theming
The attraction immerses guests in the fictional world of the Swiss Family Robinson. The treehouse is presented as a meticulously crafted home built by the family, utilizing salvaged materials from their shipwreck. The theming includes various rooms, such as a kitchen, living room, and bedrooms, all showcasing the family's ingenuity and resourcefulness. The environment is filled with details, including furniture, tools, and mechanical contraptions, all designed to enhance the narrative of survival and adaptation.

## Attraction Experience
Guests ascend the treehouse via a series of wooden staircases and rope bridges. The walk-through experience allows visitors to explore the various levels and rooms of the treehouse at their own pace. The attraction features numerous visual details, including water wheels, pulley systems, and other mechanical devices, demonstrating the family's inventive spirit. The entire experience typically lasts approximately 10-15 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
The Swiss Family Treehouse is suitable for guests of all ages. There are no height restrictions. Guests should be able to navigate stairs and bridges. The attraction is a walk-through experience and does not involve any physical restraints or sudden movements.
