# Bijutsu-kan Gallery
**Location:** Epcot

## Overview
Bijutsu-kan Gallery is a cultural exhibit located within the Japan Pavilion at Epcot's World Showcase. It showcases a rotating collection of Japanese art and artifacts, providing guests with insights into the country's rich artistic heritage. The gallery is a permanent fixture of the pavilion and offers a tranquil space for exploration and appreciation of Japanese culture.

## Narrative and Theming
The gallery's theming is rooted in the traditional aesthetics of Japan. The space is designed to evoke the atmosphere of a museum or art gallery in Japan, with minimalist design elements and careful lighting to highlight the displayed artwork. The exhibit aims to present a curated selection of Japanese art, offering context and information about the pieces and their historical significance. The setting encourages quiet contemplation and appreciation of the artistry on display.

## Attraction Experience
Guests enter the gallery and proceed through a series of displays. The exhibits typically feature a variety of art forms, including paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts. Informational plaques provide details about the artists, techniques, and historical periods represented. The duration of a visit varies depending on the guest's interest and the current exhibit, but typically ranges from 15 to 30 minutes. The experience is self-guided, allowing guests to explore at their own pace.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Bijutsu-kan Gallery is suitable for all ages. There are no height restrictions or specific health warnings associated with this attraction. The gallery is accessible to guests with disabilities.
