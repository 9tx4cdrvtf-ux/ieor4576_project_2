# Festival of the Lion King
**Location:** Animal Kingdom

## Overview
Festival of the Lion King is a live stage show attraction located in the Africa section of Disney's Animal Kingdom. It is a theatrical performance celebrating the animated film *The Lion King*. The show features singers, dancers, acrobats, and puppetry, presented in a circular theater-in-the-round format. The attraction is a significant component of Animal Kingdom's entertainment offerings, providing a high-capacity, family-friendly experience.

## Narrative and Theming
The show is presented as a celebratory festival, with the audience participating as part of the festivities. The narrative follows the key moments of *The Lion King*, including the birth of Simba, his exile, and his eventual return to Pride Rock. The setting is a vibrant African savanna, with elaborate costumes and set pieces designed to evoke the film's iconic imagery. The show emphasizes themes of community, family, and the circle of life.

## Attraction Experience
Guests are seated in a large, covered theater. The performance incorporates live singing, dancing, and acrobatics. Performers utilize elaborate costumes, including those representing various animals from the film. The show includes pyrotechnics, aerial stunts, and audience participation elements. The duration of the show is approximately 30 minutes.

## Target Audience and Restrictions
Festival of the Lion King is designed for a general audience, including families with young children. There are no height restrictions. The show is suitable for guests of all ages. Guests with sensitivity to loud noises or pyrotechnics should be aware of these elements within the performance.
